Motivation: The Quest for Optimal Asset Allocation

Asset allocation seeks an optimal capital weight vector \( \mathbf{w} \) to maximize risk-adjusted performance, fundamentally formulated as the Sharpe Ratio. The objective is mathematically defined as:

\( \max_{\mathbf{w}} SharpeRatio(\mathbf{w}) = \frac{Benefit(\mathbf{w})}{Risk(\mathbf{w})} = \frac{\mathbf{w}^T \boldsymbol{\mu} - R_f}{\sqrt{\mathbf{w}^T \boldsymbol{\Sigma} \mathbf{w}}} \)

subject to \( 1^T \mathbf{w} = 1, \mathbf{w} \ge 0 \)

Where \( \boldsymbol{\mu} \) is the expected return vector, \( \boldsymbol{\Sigma} \) is the covariance matrix quantifying structural risk, and \( R_f \) is the risk-free rate.

The Traditional Flaw: Classical models rely heavily on plug-in estimators (\( \boldsymbol{\mu}, \boldsymbol{\Sigma} \)) that suffer extensively from market noise, ill-conditioning, and "false correlations".

The Machine Learning Solution: We seamlessly bridge Machine Learning and Financial Optimization by introducing Factor GCN. It serves as an end-to-end non-linear solver directly mapping market graphs to the optimal simplex \( \Delta^{N-1} \), ensuring a robust, noise-resistant portfolio allocation.

Methodology: The Factor GCN Architecture

By utilizing graph neural networks to extract dynamic, non-linear market features, we construct a robust learning framework.

STAGE 1: Factor-Based Structural Graph

Instead of relying on noisy historical return correlations, we construct a market graph from long-term fundamental factors containing firm characteristics such as Market Capitalization, ROA, and Debt Ratio.

\( A_{ij} = \exp\left(-\frac{||z_i - z_j||^2}{2\sigma^2}\right) \)

We incorporate a self-loop mechanism \( \overline{A} = A + I_N \) and compute the symmetric normalized graph Laplacian:

\( \tilde{L}_{sym} = \tilde{D}^{-1/2} \tilde{A} \tilde{D}^{-1/2} \)

This resulting normalized graph acts as a structural filter, allowing short-term signals to propagate through fundamentally similar assets while suppressing noisy price correlations.

STAGE 2: Spectral Feature Fusion

We introduce short-term momentum signals (\( X \)) and leverage the structural map (\( \tilde{L}_{sym} \)) as a spatial low-pass filter.

To extract high-level representations, we apply a learnable feature extraction matrix \( \Theta \) alongside a Sparse Activation mechanism:

\( H = SparseAct_K(\tilde{L}_{sym} X \Theta) \)

The \( SparseAct_K(\cdot) \) is a Top-K activation function that zeroes out all but the most promising K signals, maximizing the signal-to-noise ratio.

STAGE 3: End-to-End Optimization via Gradient Descent

The filtered hidden state \( H \) is projected into an investment confidence vector \( Z \) via a secondary scoring parameter \( \Phi \). These signals are then mapped into a strict capital allocation vector \( \mathbf{w} \) using a Temperature-scaled Softmax function.

\( \mathbf{w} = Softmax(Z / \tau) \)

Objective Function & Backpropagation: Instead of traditional quadratic programming, we optimize \( \Theta \) and \( \Phi \) over 500 epochs using Gradient Descent. We directly utilize the negative Sharpe Ratio as our loss function:

\( \mathcal{L}(\Theta, \Phi) = -SharpeRatio(\mathbf{w}) = -\frac{\mathbf{w}^T \boldsymbol{\mu} - R_f}{\sqrt{\mathbf{w}^T \boldsymbol{\Sigma} \mathbf{w}}} \)

The gradients of the Sharpe Ratio flow backward through the network via the Chain Rule of Calculus:

\( \nabla_{\Phi}\mathcal{L} = \frac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial \mathbf{w}} \cdot \frac{\partial \mathbf{w}}{\partial Z} \cdot \frac{\partial Z}{\partial \Phi} \)

\( \nabla_{\Theta}\mathcal{L} = \frac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial \mathbf{w}} \cdot \frac{\partial \mathbf{w}}{\partial Z} \cdot \frac{\partial Z}{\partial H} \cdot \frac{\partial H}{\partial \Theta} \)

Combined with a learning rate \( \eta \) and an Early Stopping mechanism to prevent overfitting, this enables the model to autonomously adapt to true market dynamics.

Empirical Results & Out-of-Sample Backtesting

Rigorous backtesting demonstrates Factor GCN's real-world robustness against market noise:

  • Universe & Data: Top 150 listed companies in Taiwan sourced from the TEJ Pro database.
  • Testing Period: January 2024 – May 2026 (Out-of-Sample).
  • Baselines: Market Index (0050.TW), Equal-Weight (1/N), and Hierarchical Risk Parity (HRP).
  • Execution: Monthly dynamic rebalancing under realistic constraints, with standard transaction costs fully incorporated.

Generalization and Future Work

  • Customizable Factor Inputs: The framework is highly adaptable, allowing portfolio managers to flexibly substitute the underlying structural factors to align with their specific investment mandates and risk preferences.
  • Dynamic Perception via GAT: Future iterations will integrate Graph Attention Networks (GAT) to dynamically learn and adjust edge weights, enabling real-time adaptation to sudden market regime shifts.
